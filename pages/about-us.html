Enduring Legacy of Compassion, Care & Dignity

Restoring Hope to Forgotten Lives

Since childhood, Jaskirat Singh had a deep desire to help those in need. Every Saturday and Sunday, he and his family would serve meals to the homeless near Ambala Cantt Railway Station, where countless destitute individuals struggled for survival.

One fateful day, Jaskirat Veerji encountered a man whose both hands were amputated. His family had abandoned him, leaving him to fend for himself. When Jaskirat offered him food, the man, unable to eat with his hands, bent down and ate like an animal. Witnessing this heartbreaking sight, Jaskirat Veerji fed him with his own hands. This moment deeply moved him, and he made a life-changing decision to build a home where no one would suffer or cry in helplessness.

In 2018, Jaskirat applied for a work-from-home job in IT, allowing him the flexibility to manage his professional career while also dedicating himself to humanitarian work. That same year, he transformed his own farmhouse into a shelter home, providing a safe haven for the homeless, abandoned, and mentally distressed individuals.

Throughout this journey, his mother, Balwinder Kaur, stood by him as his greatest pillar of strength. She not only encouraged his vision but also actively participated in caring for the residents, treating them as her own family.

With God's grace, today Ni Aasre Da Aasra has become a reliable sanctuary for those in need. Over 48 police stations and multiple hospitals regularly send helpless individuals to our ambulance service, ensuring that no one is left to suffer on the streets.

Our Vision

A society where no human being is left to suffer alone in hospitals, on roadsides, or in neglected corners. A world where the homeless, abandoned, mentally ill, and physically disabled are provided with care, dignity, and a second chance at life.

Our Mission

To rescue, rehabilitate, and reintegrate the most neglected individuals, those abandoned in hospitals, dying on roadsides, or left untreated. We provide shelter, food, medical care, and reconnect lost souls with their families whenever possible.

Our Approach

A blend of compassion and action, collaborating with hospitals, police, volunteers, and donors. We bridge spirituality with humanitarian service to build a system where no person is left to suffer alone.

About Ni Aasre Da Aasra

Ni Aasre Da Aasra, "A Shelter for the Shelterless", is a humanitarian NGO founded with a mission to bring hope to those forgotten by society: the destitute, homeless, mentally and physically ill, abandoned, and dying. We provide shelter, food, medical care, and rehabilitation, while actively working to reunite individuals with their families and restore their dignity.

Our Founders

The hearts behind the shelter.

Jaskirat Singh - Lovingly Called Veer Ji

Founder

A compassionate IT professional turned humanitarian, Jaskirat Singh transformed his personal farmhouse into a shelter home in 2018. Driven by a life-changing encounter with a helpless soul near Ambala Railway Station, he dedicated his life to ensuring no one suffers alone. Today, he leads Ni Aasre Da Aasra as a sanctuary of hope for the destitute and abandoned.

Balwinder Kaur - Lovingly Called Mata Ji

Co-Founder & Pillar of Strength

The nurturing heart behind Ni Aasre Da Aasra, Balwinder Kaur stood beside her son Jaskirat from day one. She personally cares for every resident, treating them as her own family. Her unwavering support, tireless service, and motherly affection have shaped this shelter into the warm, compassionate home it is today.
